Who Are the Main Audience of Your Ethical Analysis:

The audience includes policymakers, business owners, engineers, scientists, and everyday users. Policymakers make the rules for AI, business owners use it in their work, engineers and scientists develop the technology, and citizens use it in daily life. Each group plays a role in ensuring AI like ChatGPT is used responsibly and ethically, especially to address risks like misinformation, bias, and job loss.

Why You Should Care:

ChatGPT affects society by improving productivity and access to information but can cause issues like spreading false information or replacing jobs. Policymakers must create fair rules, businesses need to use AI responsibly, developers should fix problems like bias, and users must think critically about AI outputs. Everyone should work together to reduce risks and make AI safe and fair for everyone.

Recommendations to the Stakeholders Who Use and Deploy the Proposed Technology:

Policymakers should create privacy laws and rules to stop harmful AI use. Businesses should be clear when they use AI, train workers, and use AI to help not replace people. Engineers must build systems that are fair, explainable, and accurate. Scientists should research ways to detect and fix bias. Citizens need to learn about AI, question its results, and push for ethical use
